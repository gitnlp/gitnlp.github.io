Machine Reading Comprehension

  1. Machine Comprehension Using Match-LSTM and Answer Pointer. Shuohang Wang and Jing Jiang. ICLR 2017.
    Match-LSTM #squad
  2. Dynamic Coattention Networks For Question Answering. Caiming Xiong, Victor Zhong, and Richard Socher. ICLR 2017.
    DCN #squad
  3. Gated Self-Matching Networks for Reading Comprehension and Question Answering. Wenhui Wang, Nan Yang, Furu Wei, Baobao Chang and Ming Zhou. ACL 2017.
    r-net #squad
  4. Fast and Accurate Reading Comprehension by Combining Self-Attention and Convolution. Adams Wei Yu, David Dohan, Thang Luong, Rui Zhao, Kai Chen, and Quoc Le. ICLR 2018.
    Data augmentation by backtranslation #squad
  5. S-Net: From Answer Extraction to Answer Generation for Machine Reading Comprehension. Chuanqi Tan, Furu Wei, Nan Yang, Bowen Du, Weifeng Lv, and Ming Zhou. AAAI 2018.
    s-net #marco
  6. Context-Aware Answer Sentence Selection With Hierarchical Gated Recurrent Neural Networks. Chuanqi Tan, Furu Wei, Nan Yang, Bowen Du, Weifeng Lv, and Ming Zhou. IEEE/ACM Transactions on Audio, Speech and Language Processing. 2018.
    #text_qa #passage_ranking #sentence_ranking
  7. Enhanced LSTM for Natural Language Inference. Qian Chen, Xiaodan Zhu, Zhen-Hua Ling, Si Wei, Hui Jiang, and Diana Inkpen. ACL 2017.
    #esim #snli #sentence_ranking
  8. Simple and Effective Multi-Paragraph Reading Comprehension. Christopher Clark and Matt Gardner. ACL 2017.
    #no-answer-option

Pretraining NNs for NLP

  1. A Neural Probabilistic Language Model.. Yoshua Bengio, Réjean Ducharme, Pascal Vincent, and Christian Jauvin. JMLR 2003.
  2. Natural Language Processing (Almost) from Scratch.. Ronan Collobert, Jason Weston, Leon Bottou, Michael Karlen, Koray Kavukcuoglu, and Pavel Kuksa. JMLR 2011.
  3. GloVe: Global Vectors for Word Representation. Jeffrey Pennington, Richard Socher, and Christopher D. Manning. EMNLP 2014. GloVe@StanfordNLP.

  4. Distributed Representations of Words and Phrases and their Compositionality. Tomas Mikolov, Ilya Sutskever, Kai Chen, Greg Corrado, and Jeffrey Dean. NIPS 2013. code.
    Pretrained word embeddings. #word2vec
  5. Dependency-Based Word Embeddings. Rafal Jozefowicz, Oriol Vinyals, Mike Schuster, Noam Shazeer, and Yonghui Wu. ACL 2014.
    Pretrained word embeddings.
    6. List of pretrained word embeddings.
  6. Exploring the Limits of Language Modeling. Rafal Jozefowicz, Oriol Vinyals, Mike Schuster, Noam Shazeer, and Yonghui Wu. ICLR 2016.
    Pretrained RNNs for natural language processing!
  7. Deep Contextualized Word Representations. Matthew E. Peters, Mark Neumann, Mohit Iyyer, Matt Gardner, Christopher Clark, Kenton Lee and Luke Zettlemoyer. ELMo@AllenNLP. ICLR 2017.
    Pretrained RNNs works for machine reading and other NLP tasks!
  8. Improving Language Understanding by Generative Pre-Training. Alec Radford, Karthik Narasimhan, Tim Salimans, and Ilya Sutskever. arXiv 2018.
    Pretrained NNs for natural language processing! #transformer
  9. BERT: Pre-training of Deep Bidirectional Transformers for Language Understanding. Jacob Devlin, Ming-Wei Chang, Kenton Lee, and Kristina Toutanova. arXiv 2018.
    Pretrained NNs for natural language processing! #bert #transformer
  10. GLoMo: Unsupervisedly Learned Relational Graphs as Transferable Representations. Zhilin Yang, Jake Zhao, Bhuwan Dhingra, Kaiming He, William W. Cohen, Ruslan Salakhutdinov, and Yann LeCun. 2018.
    Pretrained RNNs for natural language processing! #transformer #pretrained_self_attention
  11. Neural Machine Translation by Jointly Learning to Align and Translate. Dzmitry Bahdanau, Kyunghyun Cho, and Yoshua Bengio. ICLR 2015. slides.
    #nmt #seq2seq
  12. Attention Is All You Need. Ashish Vaswani, Noam Shazeer, Niki Parmar, Jakob Uszkoreit, Llion Jones, and Aidan N. Gomez, Lukasz Kaiser, Illia Polosukhin. NIPS 2017. Tensor2Tensor.
    #nmt #seq2seq #transformer
  13. The Annotated Transformer. harvardnlp.
    #transformer
